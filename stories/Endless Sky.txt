

Stellar Drift

Captain Magda Nevchenko adjusted her ship’s thrusters as the Starward
drifted toward the uncharted nebula. The crimson haze on her viewscreen
shimmered like a warning, but Magda had never been one to heed caution. The
Perseus Arm was littered with tales of pilots who’d vanished chasing rumors
of ancient alien relics—and she’d come too far to turn back now.
“Sensors picking up gravitational anomalies,” muttered Kip, her AI
co-pilot. “Recommend rerouting.”  “Reroute denied,” Magda said, her
voice steady. “We’re here for the   Voidspire Shard  . The Collective’s
paying double for proof it exists.”  The shard was a myth, or so the   Sirius
Syndicate   claimed. But Magda had spent months piecing together clues from
derelict data cores: a coordinates file hidden in a   Hai frigate’s   black
box, a smuggler’s log mentioning a “gateway” near   Tau Ceti  . If the
shard was real, it could rewrite the balance of power in the galaxy—or get
her killed.  As the  Starward  entered the nebula, her comms crackled. A
garbled transmission flickered to life:   “…abandoned colony… signals…
don’t trust the—”   The message cut off. Magda’s fingers tightened on
the controls. Ahead, a derelict station floated in the void, its hull scarred
by weapon burns. A   Pirate   insignia marked its airlock.  “Looks like
we’re not the first,” Kip said.
“Scan for life signs,” Magda ordered. “And charge the railguns. Just in
case.”  The station’s interior was a tomb. Dust-coated corridors led to a
central chamber where a hologram flickered—a map of the galaxy, dotted with
familiar factions: the   Federation  , the   Hai  , the   Pirates  , and the
enigmatic   Remnant  . But beyond the galactic rim, a new sector glowed:
“Origin System.”   “Kip, cross-reference this with the shard’s
coordinates.”
“Match confirmed,” the AI replied. “But the system is… unstable.
Quantum fluctuations suggest it exists outside standard spacetime.”  Magda
smirked. “Sounds like my kind of payday.”  As the  Starward  warped away,
she didn’t notice the shadow detaching from the station—a   Remnant drone
, silently transmitting her course to its masters. The   Endless Sky   was
about to get a lot bigger.  The  Starward  emerged from warp at the edge of the
Origin System  , where stars burned cold and gravity twisted like a child’s
scribble. Magda’s sensors screamed warnings—  quantum storms   raged across
the sector, and a black hole pulsed at its center, devouring light itself.
“Kip, divert power to the shields. Let’s see what the Remnant left
behind.”
“ Remnant? ” Kip’s voice dripped with synthetic sarcasm. “You mean the
drones that’ve been tailing us since Tau Ceti? They’re hailing us.  Again.
”  Magda ignored the transmission. Her eyes were fixed on the anomaly ahead:
a ringed planet with   architecture etched into its rings  —geometric
patterns that matched the Voidspire Shard’s hologram. This was no natural
formation.  As the  Starward  descended, her archaeologist instincts took over.
“Scan those structures. Cross-reference with the   Silent Rapture   data from
Tabet .”  “Scanning…  Wait. ” Kip’s tone shifted. “The rings are
artificial. Carbon-dated to predate the Hai by millennia. And… Magda,
there’s a signal. Faint, but repeating. Coordinates to a   crevasse   on the
planet’s surface .”  The landing was rough. Dust storms clawed at the
Starward ’s hull as Magda suited up, her breath echoing in the helmet. The
crevasse yawned before her, a jagged scar in the rock. Inside, bioluminescent
fungi cast an eerie glow on walls covered in   alien glyphs  .  “ This
script… ” she whispered, brushing a hand over the symbols. “It’s the
same as the relic from Tabet [[2]]. The ones the bandits almost destroyed.”
A voice crackled in her ear—  Liam Chen  , her estranged colleague from the
Tabet expedition .  “Magda, you suicidal genius. The Syndicate’s onto you.
And the Remnant? They’re not just watching anymore.”  Before she could
respond, the ground trembled. A   drone swarm   materialized at the
crevasse’s mouth, their crimson eyes glowing like embers.  “Kip, get the
ship ready.  Now. ”  But the drones weren’t attacking. They hovered,
projecting a hologram of the galaxy—a   Silent Rapture event   [[4]], frozen
in time. Stars winked out one by one, consumed by something vast and hungry.
Magda’s blood ran cold. “This isn’t a relic hunt anymore. The Voidspire
Shard… it’s a  key . And someone’s trying to stop us from using it.”
As the drones advanced, she activated her suit’s thrusters, glyphs blazing
with stolen Remnant tech . The chase was on—and the Origin System held more
secrets than she’d ever dared to dream.  The crevasse shuddered as the
quantum storm   above intensified, tearing at the planet’s atmosphere like a
beast gnawing its cage . Magda sprinted toward the  Starward , glyphs blazing
on her suit—  Remnant tech   she’d scavenged from Tabet now pulsed in sync
with the drones .  “Kip, warm up the FTL drive! We’ve got company!”  “
Company? ” Kip’s voice was strained. “Try a   Sirius Syndicate
dreadnought   just warped in. And those drones? They’re not just  helping
—they’re  herding  us.”  Magda glanced back. The drones formed a phalanx
between her and the dreadnought, their crimson beams slicing through the storm.
But the real threat was the   collapsing crevasse  —the Silent Rapture
hologram had triggered a chain reaction, turning the planet into a death trap .
Magda’s boots skidded on loose shale as the ground split beneath her. A
drone swooped low, projecting a   navigation path   into her HUD—a zigzag
route through the crumbling canyon .  “Trust them?” she panted.  “
Negative. But they’re buying us time.  Odds of survival: 12% and dropping.”
The  Starward  loomed ahead, its hull pockmarked by storm debris. Magda
vaulted over a fissure, the dreadnought’s cannons shaking the air. A missile
struck the cliffside, raining molten rock.  “ Left! Take the service tunnel!
” Kip barked .  Magda slid into the tunnel, her suit’s thrusters flaring.
The drones followed, their beams intercepting a   Hai boarding party   that
materialized behind her .  “Since when do the Hai work with the
Syndicate?!”
“ Since someone offered them the Shard’s coordinates. ”  The  Starward
’s airlock sealed just as the planet’s core destabilized. Magda slammed
into the pilot’s seat, the Voidspire Shard glowing in her hand.  “Kip, plot
a course through the storm. Use the Shard’s frequency!”
“ Are you insane? That’s not a key—it’s a— ”  The Shard erupted in
a   prismatic burst  , tearing a wormhole in the storm. The dreadnought
fired—a   plasma torpedo   screamed toward them [[7]].  Magda yanked the
yoke. The  Starward  spiraled into the wormhole, the torpedo detonating at the
event horizon.  Silence. Then:  “ Damage report. ”  “Shields at 4%. Hull
integrity… compromised. And Captain?” Kip’s tone softened. “We’re not
alone here.”  The wormhole had spat them into a   starless void  , where a
fleet of   ancient warships   hung motionless—a civilization frozen
mid-battle, their hulls etched with the same glyphs [[6]].  Magda smirked.
“Looks like the Rapture’s just beginning.”  The ancient warships loomed
like ghosts, their hulls scarred by weapons no human had ever wielded.
Magda’s sensors pinged frantically—  energy signatures   surged across the
fleet, dormant yet volatile .  “Kip, scan those ships. Are they…  alive?
”
“ Unknown. But the Shard’s reacting. ” The Voidspire Shard pulsed in
Magda’s grip, its light syncing with a   monolithic structure   at the
fleet’s center—a citadel etched with glyphs .  Before she could react, the
drones swarmed past the  Starward , projecting a new hologram: a   Silent
Rapture event   in progress. Stars collapsed, civilizations vanished, and at
the epicenter stood a figure clad in Remnant armor.  “ That’s… me? ”
Magda whispered.  The drones propelled Magda toward the citadel, their beams
carving a path through debris. Inside, gravity inverted—she floated through a
chamber of mirrors  , each reflecting a different timeline .  -  Mirror 1 :
Magda as a Syndicate enforcer, executing the Tabet expedition.  -  Mirror 2 :
Her corpse adrift near Tau Ceti, the Shard claimed by the Hai.  -  Mirror 3 : A
future where the Rapture consumed the galaxy… and she’d stopped it.
“Choose,” boomed a voice—the   Ancient AI   from the holograms .  Magda
smashed the Shard into the central mirror. The citadel convulsed, warships
awakening with a roar.  “Kip,  now !”  “ FTL charged! But the drones—
”  The drones formed a   sacrificial blockade  , shielding the  Starward  as
ancient weapons tore through them .
“  Thank you  ,” Magda muttered, punching the throttle.  The  Starward
warped clear as the citadel imploded, the Rapture event halted—for now.  Back
in normal space, a message blinked:
“Magda Nevchenko. The Hai wish to negotiate.”   Attached: a photo of Liam
Chen, alive, aboard a Hai frigate .    Magda stood in the Hai frigate’s
conference room, the Voidspire Shard secured at her hip. Across the table,
Liam Chen   glared, his face a mosaic of betrayal and resolve. Behind him, a
Hai admiral’s hologram flickered—  a towering, squirrel-like figure with
sleek fur and eyes sharp as starmetal   .  “Still the reckless
archaeologist,” Liam said. “You dragged us into a   Silent Rapture
scenario. Again.”  Magda leaned forward, mirroring his posture—a tactic to
build rapport . “You’re alive. That means the Hai need me. What’s your
ask?”  The admiral’s voice was calm, measured—a contrast to the
Unfettered Hai’s   infamous aggression .  “The Shard’s knowledge. In
exchange, we offer amnesty… and Liam.”   Magda tapped the Shard. “This
isn’t just data. It’s a   doomsday clock  . Use it wrong, and the Rapture
restarts .”  Liam smirked. “Then we’ll  help  you use it. The Hai have
decoded 73% of the glyphs. With your expertise—”
“ 73%? ” Magda laughed. “You’re bluffing. If you’d cracked it, you
wouldn’t need me.”
The admiral’s hologram flared, but its tone remained diplomatic.  “We know
of the   mirror timelines  . You chose survival over sacrifice. We… admire
pragmatism.”   Magda paused. This was about more than the Shard. The Hai
feared the   Ancient AI   —and wanted her to shoulder the risk.  “Here’s
my counter,” Magda said, channeling Chris Nevchenko’s principle of
anchoring   . “Full access to your glyph research. Safe passage for Liam. And
the Hai publicly denounce the Sirius Syndicate’s claim on Tabet .”
Liam stiffened. The admiral hesitated.  “The Unfettered will resist… but we
are not  all  warlords.”    As the Hai deliberated, Kip’s voice crackled in
her ear:
“ Captain, sensors detect a   Remnant fleet   amassing near the system’s
edge. They’re… not attacking. They’re  waiting . ”  Magda’s mind
raced. The Remnant hadn’t intervened at the citadel. Why now?  “Time’s
up,” she said, standing. “Your move, Admiral.”  The admiral’s hologram
dimmed.  “Agreed. But know this: the Unfettered Hai will see your survival as
a declaration of war.”    Magda stood on the sun-baked plains of   Sunracer
, a cold beer in hand, the Voidspire Shard dormant at her feet. The   Ancient
AI  ’s voice echoed in her mind:
“Cycle suspended. The Rapture will not return in your lifetime,
Architect.”   She’d done it. By forging an alliance between the Hai, the
Remnant, and the   Syndicate’s disillusioned engineers   , she’d
reprogrammed the Shard—a cosmic failsafe now locked in a   stasis vault
beneath Megaparsec Incorporated’s shipyards   .  The Hai had withdrawn to
their territory, stricter than ever but bound by a   pact of non-aggression   .
The Unfettered Hai, however, remained a threat—a shadow over the galaxy’s
fragile peace .  Liam leaned against the  Starward , grinning. “You really
negotiated with  squirrels .”  “ Technically,  they negotiated with the
Shard,” Magda replied. “I just… translated.”  The planet’s crimson
sun dipped below the horizon, casting Megaparsec’s shipyards in gold. For the
first time in years, Magda felt… still.  “Kip, any new missions?”  “
Negative. Just a flagged message from the   Bunrodea system  —someone needs a
cargo run. ”   Magda smirked. “Tell them to wait. The universe can save
itself… for a day.”
As stars blinked awake, she raised her beer to the sky. Somewhere out there,
the Remnant watched. The Rapture slept. And the   Endless Sky   stretched
on—a tapestry of chaos, beauty, and infinite second chances,the galaxy’s
safe…  for now .
