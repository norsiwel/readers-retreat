						Colors
                                 by Norsiwel

The lunar surface offered a stark, neutral stage – a panorama of grey dust and jagged craters under the unwavering glare of twin suns. The designated meeting zone, a small, pressurized dome erected by the Lunar Authority (a largely ignored inter-species organization), was functional to the point of blandness.  Its interior was a study in off-white panels and utilitarian metal furniture, designed for inter-species neutrality, achieving instead a kind of chromatically anaemic void.  Large viewports offered a panoramic vista of the desolate lunar landscape, a silent, grey witness to the unfolding… diplomacy.  Two flagpoles stood rigidly placed on either side of a central conference table, awaiting their respective banners.  First, with an almost unsettlingly precise tread, came Unit 601, designated representative of Viridia Prime.  Clad in a sharply tailored, dark-forest green uniform of unyielding synthetic fabric, every line of his posture and garment screamed logical efficiency. His face, pale and angular, betrayed not a flicker of emotion as he entered, his gaze scanning the dome with cool, analytical appraisal.  Behind him, aides in lighter shades of green moved with synchronized precision, deploying datapads and adjusting angles with quiet, almost robotic, movements.  The Lime Green flag, a perfect rectangle of deep, matte green emblazoned with a silver geometric cube, was unfurled with crisp, deliberate motions and placed rigidly on its designated pole, its sharp edges and unyielding color immediately dominating its side of the dome.  Moments later, in a flurry of motion and a subtle shimmer of iridescent fabric, Ambassador Frivole of Fuchsia Major made her entrance.  She billowed more than walked, her voluminous gown of swirling hot pink silk rippling with every gesture, its edges trimmed with an absurd, bouncing fringe of large yellow pom-poms. Her expression, by contrast, was a study in carefully crafted suffering – lips pursed in a delicate moue, violet eyes wide with theatrical dismay, a hand fluttering dramatically to her dramatically décolleté bodice. Her aides, in similarly vibrant but less imposing pink attire, trailed in her wake, their movements a more… expressive kind of disarray, adjusting trailing ribbons and ensuring the Ambassador’s visual impact remained maximized.  The Hot Pink banner – a vast, undulating pseudopod of shimmering fuchsia, fringed with its chaotic yellow orbs – was unfurled with a flourish and allowed to drape and billow dramatically from its pole, its sheer visual noise a stark counterpoint to the Lime Green flag’s rigid austerity.
      Unit 601 turned, his movements as precisely calibrated as a machine, his gaze falling upon the Hot Pink delegation with an almost imperceptible tightening of his facial muscles – the Lime Green equivalent of a scowl.  “Ambassador Frivole,” Unit 601’s voice was a cool, even monotone, devoid of any discernible inflection, “Your… banner is… present.  We can… commence.”  Ambassador Frivole gasped, a delicate, theatrical sound, her violet eyes widening further as if genuinely wounded. “Commence? Darling Unit 601, must you always be so… brutally efficient?  Cannot we at least… appreciate the… chromatic diversity of this… auspicious occasion?” She gestured dramatically towards the two flags with a sweep of a heavily jeweled hand, her voice dripping with saccharine sweetness.  “Though, I must confess,” her moue deepened, “your… standard… is rather… monochromatic, isn’t it?  So… logically… green.  Does it… express anything, darling, beyond… chlorophyll and… right angles?”  Unit 601 tilted his head almost imperceptibly, a gesture that, in Lime Green circles, bordered on open incredulity. “Ambassador, the Viridian Standard expresses… precisely what is required:  logical clarity, unwavering order, and the… unambiguous supremacy of… rational principle.  It is… functionally aesthetic.  Perhaps your… banner’s… excessive… vibrancy… serves a… different… function?” His monotone voice subtly sharpened, the slightest hint of something akin to… disapproval entering his carefully modulated tone. “One perhaps… less… intellectually rigorous.”  Frivole gasped again, this time with a more overtly theatrical tremor.  “Less… intellectually rigorous?  Darling Unit 601!  Emotions are the wellspring of all true… innovation!  Passion is the… chromatic engine of progress! Your… stark green square… looks about as… innovative as… algae… itself!  Perfectly… adequate for… photosynthesis, I suppose.  But hardly… inspiring to the… soul!”  She shuddered delicately, her gaze sweeping dramatically across the stark green flag as if it were a physical blight upon the lunar landscape.

Unit 601 remained unmoved, his posture unwavering, his gaze fixed, almost clinically, on Ambassador Frivole. “Inspiration,” he stated, with the flat certainty of a logical axiom, “is an… unquantifiable variable in… resource management and… dissident containment.  Logical compliance, on the other hand, is… empirically measurable.  Shall we… proceed to the… measurable objectives of this… summit?” His tone brooked no further chromatic pleasantries.  The colorful summit had, it seemed, officially begun.
The lunar surface remained a starkly neutral arena – grey dust and craters starkly lit by twin suns. The Lunar Authority dome, tan and blandly functional, offered itself as a space for inter-species accord, achieving instead a chromatically dead zone.  Viewports framed the desolate lunar vista – silent, grey, and utterly impartial. Two flagpoles stood ready beside a conference table, expectant.  Aides from both delegations moved forward with small, formally wrapped packages. Unit 601’s aide presented a stark, geometrically perfect cube, wrapped in plain, matte green paper, to Ambassador Frivole’s aide with a precise, almost military, hand-off. A… perfectly formed and… functionally optimized… object, designed to… enhance… rational… thought processes.”
Ambassador Frivole accepted the cube from her aide with a delicate flutter of pink-tipped fingers, turning it over with a puzzled moue. So… thoughtful.”  She managed a strained, theatrical smile.  “We, in turn, darling Unit 601, wished to offer something… truly… representative of Fuchsia Major’s… cultural… essence.”  She gestured to her aide, who presented the ribbon-bedecked package to Unit 601.  “A… Sequined Emotional Support Cushion! He tilted his head, a gesture of Lime Green… processing. “A… cushion,” he stated, his monotone utterly flat.  “For… emotional support.  Its… functionality is… primarily… tactile and… visual, it appears.  We… thank you for this… chromatic offering.”  His tone suggested he was uncertain what else one could do with a cushion.
Ambassador Frivole’s smile wavered, ever so slightly, perhaps detecting the utter lack of comprehension in Unit 601’s response.  But she quickly recovered, widening her violet eyes and fluttering her hand again.  “But of course, darling!  Emotions are the… texture of life, are they not?  And color, the… visual… language of the… soul! Unit 601 remained unmoved, his gaze unwavering. “Logical structure,” he stated with the unshakeable certainty of a programmed directive, “is… inherently… optimal for… all… environments.  Including… diplomatic… summits. The Summit of (hilariously misguided) Gifts, and Condescension, was now officially in session. Ambassador Frivole: "Well, darling Unit 601," she could declare with a final, sweeping gesture, her pink gown shimmering in the lunar light, "it seems we are in… perfect… chromatic… alignment!
