                               Night Sky

The Zenthari bodies sat motionless in their crystalline alcoves, rows of
slender forms arranged like statues in the vast Hall of Stillness. Their
physical forms remained rooted to the nutrient-rich soil of their homeworld,
delicate tendrils absorbing sustenance while their projected selves roamed the
physical spaces beyond these walls. Only the gentle pulsing of bioluminescent
veins along their temples revealed any sign of life within these stationary
vessels.

Kaelen's consciousness condensed in the central chamber, forming a shimmering
approximation of his physical body. He watched his hands solidify from mist
into something that could grasp objects, though he chose to keep them slightly
translucent today. Across the room, Lyssa's projection flickered as she passed
through the stone wall, her form shifting from solid to ethereal and back again.

"Your projection stability is poor," Kaelen observed, noting how Lyssa's edges
blurred at the corners when she grew anxious. He extended his hand, the skin
becoming fully opaque as he tested the solidity, feeling the cool resistance of
his own projected flesh.

Lyssa's fingers trembled as she deliberately solidified them to grasp the
ceremonial cup on the table. "The Outliers grow bolder. Three more chose
physical movement yesterday. They severed their root-tendrils." The cup rattled
against the stone surface, betraying her emotional state through the physical
vibration.

Kaelen stepped through the chamber door without opening it, his form dissolving
momentarily before reforming on the other side. He preferred this method over
using the archway, finding the transition soothing. "The Council convenes at
the next light-cycle," he said, watching Lyssa struggle to maintain her
solidity. "They consider the severing protocol for those who choose physical
movement."

"The severing protocol means cutting off all connection," Lyssa replied, her
projection flickering violently as she accidentally phased halfway through the
floor. She forced herself solid again with visible effort, the scent of ozone
filling the air as her energy field stabilized. "We've never used it on our own
kind before."

Kaelen moved to the window, his hands becoming insubstantial as they passed
through the transparent crystal. Beyond, in the physical world, he could see
the Outliers' gathering place—a space where Zenthari bodies actually walked,
their tendrils severed, their movements clumsy and dangerous.

"I've been visiting the Outliers in their physical space," Lyssa admitted, her
projection suddenly becoming fully solid as if bracing for impact. "They're not
dangerous. They're... curious. They dream of touching the stars with their
hands."

Kaelen watched a group of Outliers below struggling to manipulate physical
tools, their movements jerky and uncoordinated. The Zenthari had perfected
their projection over millennia, able to interact with physical objects when
necessary but free from the limitations of constant physicality. The Outliers
had abandoned this balance entirely.

"They plan to sever me at the Council meeting," Lyssa confessed, her form
becoming semi-transparent with distress. "I've visited the Outliers too many
times. They say I've become contaminated with their ideas."

Kaelen considered his options. To visit Lyssa in the physical world would
require maintaining full projection stability for an extended period—a
difficult feat that risked draining his physical body's reserves. He could
continue to interact with her through the safer, more controlled projections
within their shared spaces, but that would mean abandoning her to the Council's
judgment.

Below, an Outlier stumbled and fell, the physical impact sending a jarring
vibration through the ground that Kaelen felt even in his projected state. The
sight made his decision clear.

Kaelen focused his energy, solidifying his entire form with deliberate
precision. He would maintain full physical interaction capability throughout
his journey to the Outliers' gathering place. He would visit Lyssa in the
physical world, despite the risk to his own standing and physical health.

Kaelen's projected form shimmered as he stepped beyond the boundary of the Hall
of Stillness, the transition from controlled environment to wild terrain
immediate. His feet registered the uneven ground through the solid projection,
each stone sending a jarring sensation up his legs. Maintaining full
physicality required constant energy expenditure—he could feel the drain in
his stationary body's reserves, measured by the fading pulse of his temple
veins back in the Hall.

He passed through a grove of crystalline trees, deliberately keeping his form
solid as he navigated around their physical trunks. An Outlier child ran past,
her actual body stumbling over roots, her movements jerky and uncoordinated.
Kaelen watched her struggle to grasp a branch, her fingers fumbling with the
physical reality the Zenthari had abandoned millennia ago.

The gathering place came into view—a clearing where Outliers moved their
physical bodies with painstaking effort. Some practiced lifting objects, others
attempted to coordinate their movements. Kaelen noted the exhaustion in their
postures, the way their real bodies trembled from the unfamiliar exertion. A
group worked together to construct a simple shelter, their movements
synchronized through years of practice but still painfully slow compared to
mental projection.

Kaelen approached a stone bench and sat, testing his projection's stability.
The solid form held, but the energy cost spiked as he maintained contact with
the physical surface. A bead of sweat formed on his projected forehead—the
first time he'd ever simulated such a physical response.

Lyssa appeared at the clearing's edge, her actual body moving with more grace
than the others. She carried a woven basket filled with physical objects:
stones, leaves, a piece of fabric. Her eyes widened when she saw Kaelen's solid
form.

"You maintained full projection," she said, her voice rough from actual vocal
cord use. She set down the basket, her fingers trembling as they brushed
against the fabric. The scent of crushed leaves filled the air as she handled
them with deliberate care.

Kaelen stood to meet her, feeling the strain of maintaining solidity increase
with movement. "The Council plans to sever you," he projected, the words
forming in the air between them as colored light—the Zenthari method of
private communication even in physical space.

Lyssa's hands stilled on the fabric, her knuckles whitening with the physical
pressure. "Severing means what we've discovered dies with me."

"What have you discovered?" Kaelen asked, the energy drain making his
projection flicker at the edges.

Lyssa lifted a smooth stone from her basket. "Physical movement isn't just
curiosity. It changes how we think. Our projections have become stagnant,
predictable. But when we move physically..." She demonstrated by tossing the
stone into the air and catching it. "New neural pathways form. We're evolving
in ways the projected mind cannot."

A group of Outliers gathered nearby, their actual bodies forming a protective
circle. One held a device that pulsed with unstable energy—a physical
manifestation of their mental projections, crude but functional.

Kaelen felt the drain intensify, his temple veins pulsing faster as his
stationary body struggled to maintain this level of projection. The Council
would consider this gathering place dangerous, but Lyssa's discovery suggested
something deeper—that the Zenthari had stagnated by rejecting physical
movement entirely.

As Lyssa reached for his hand, Kaelen made his choice. He allowed his
projection to become insubstantial, passing through her grasp to avoid the
physical contact that would drain him further. Instead, he solidified just
enough to place a single physical object in her palm—a small crystal from the
Hall of Stillness, a token of his true self.

Kaelen's projection flickered violently as a wave of dizziness washed over
him—the first sign his energy reserves were nearing critical depletion. His
temple veins pulsed erratically, visible even through his solid form, the
rhythm growing weaker with each beat. He hadn't anticipated the solar flare
that had just passed over Zenthara, its energy disrupting the delicate balance
required to maintain physical projection.

"Your form is destabilizing faster than normal," Lyssa said, her actual hands
reaching out but stopping short of touching him. The Outliers nearby had
gathered protective crystals around the perimeter, their surfaces glowing with
absorbed solar energy. "We've been tracking the flares. They interfere with
projection stability."

Kaelen tried to step back but found his feet partially phased into the ground,
the lower half of his legs becoming insubstantial. Maintaining full solidity
during a solar event required exponentially more energy—his stationary body's
reserves were draining at three times the normal rate. He could feel the pull
of his rooted form growing stronger, the Hall of Stillness calling him back
before complete system failure occurred.

A young Outlier stumbled toward them, her physical movements unsteady as she
carried a crystal device emitting erratic pulses. "The flare's intensity is
increasing," she reported, her voice rough from disuse. "Projection stability
window closing in seventeen minutes."

Lyssa's eyes held Kaelen's gaze, her actual body trembling with the effort of
maintaining physical presence. "Seventeen minutes is barely enough time to
reach the Hall. You'll destabilize completely before then."

Kaelen's projection flickered again, this time losing solidity in his right
arm. He watched as his hand passed through the crystal Lyssa had given him, the
physical connection broken. The Council's severing plan suddenly felt trivial
compared to this immediate crisis—the solar flares represented a threat to
all Zenthari, projected or stationary.

The young Outlier placed her device on the ground, its pulsing light creating a
temporary stability field. "This buys five minutes. Maybe seven if you conserve
energy."

Kaelen made his decision. Instead of conserving energy to return safely, he
focused his remaining reserves into solidifying both hands completely. He took
the crystal from Lyssa and pressed it against his chest, leaving an imprint of
his physical signature—the Zenthari equivalent of a written message, only
possible through direct physical contact.

"Tell them the flares aren't random," he said, his voice gaining texture as his
projection stabilized momentarily through sheer will. "The pattern matches the
ancient records of the Collapse. This is how it began before the Great
Stillness."

His left leg dissolved completely, leaving him half-substantial. The Hall's
pull became overwhelming, the safety of his rooted body beckoning him back. But
Kaelen focused on one final action—he placed his solid hand on Lyssa's
shoulder, transferring not just his physical signature but a fragment of his
mental pattern, something that would survive even if his projection failed
completely.

Kaelen's consciousness snapped back to his stationary body with the force of a
physical impact. His temple veins pulsed erratically, the bioluminescent

The nutrient-rich soil beneath him felt suddenly alien—too solid, too
immediate—after the fluid reality of projection. His root-tendrils trembled

matrix.

Kaelen's physical eyes fluttered open, his temple veins still pulsing weakly.
He tried to project, but his reserves were depleted—his consciousness
